Carrots Promote Healthy Vision

Just one large carrot (one cup) can provide 100% of the daily target for vitamin A. This important nutrient (which acts as a cell-protective antioxidant) may even help protect against cancer, age-related macular degeneration, and measles. A deficiency of vitamin A can lead to a condition called xerophthalmia, which can damage normal vision and result in night blindness.

The vitamin A you get from carrots comes from two carotenoids called alpha-carotene and beta-carotene. But these aren't the only nutrients in carrots that are important for vision. The antioxidants lutein and zeaxanthin found in carrots also enhance eye health. These two natural compounds protect the retina and lens. National Institutes of Health. Vitamin A and carotenoids.

Carrots Balance Your Blood Sugar

Although carrots are known to be higher in sugar compared to other vegetables, they do have anti-diabetic properties, which was reported in a review published in Food and Nutrition Sciences. The review cites that people who had lower levels of carotenoids—the pigments that give carrots its orange color—had higher blood sugar levels and higher fasting levels of insulin. This suggests that carotenoids might help in the management of diabetes.

The soluble fiber in carrots has been shown to help regulate blood sugar and insulin levels after eating. Raw or slightly cooked carrots are also low on the glycemic index, which helps them provide a steady energy supply.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Distract yourself
Cravings last anywhere from 5-20 minutes, so get involved in some activity. Listen to your favourite music, strike up a conversation with a friend or workmate, do some chores or go for a run. Anything to get your mind off the craving.
To start a healthier diet and avoid pizza and burgers, plan meals with whole foods, cook at home, control portions, stay hydrated, and choose healthy snacks. Limit processed foods, seek support, and practice moderation with treats. Patience and small sustainable changes are crucial for success in adopting healthier eating habits.
To kickstart a healthy diet, start by setting clear, realistic goals. Assess your current eating habits and make small changes gradually. Focus on incorporating more whole foods like fruits , vegetables and whole grains into your meals.
When you're in a situation where friends are indulging in junk food, it can be challenging to stick to your healthy eating goals. To resist temptation, consider eating a nutritious snack before meeting up. Communicate your health objectives to your friends; they may offer support. Opt for healthier choices if dining out and keep yourself occupied with conversation or activities to distract from the junk food. Bringing your own healthy snacks is another option. If you do decide to indulge, do so mindfully, savoring each bite. Lastly, remind yourself of the reasons why you're pursuing a healthier diet to stay motivated and focused on your goals.
The time it takes to build healthier dietary habits can vary greatly from person to person and depends on factors such as individual preferences, lifestyle, motivation, and the extent of changes being made. Generally, forming new habits, including dietary ones, is thought to take around 21 to 66 days, according to various studies and theories on habit formation.
It's important to approach the process of developing healthier dietary habits with patience, persistence, and a focus on gradual, sustainable changes rather than seeking rapid results. Additionally, seeking support from friends, family, or healthcare professionals can be beneficial in staying motivated and accountable during the journey toward better nutrition.
